Literature Review Evaluation

Critical evaluation of the literature review submission on state-sponsored cyberterrorism.

Download Literature Review (PDF)

Learning Outcomes Addressed

  • Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.
  • Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the applicable computing field.
  • Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.

Evaluation of the Literature Review

Knowledge and Understanding: The literature review demonstrated clear understanding of the complexities surrounding state-sponsored cyberterrorism, especially issues of attribution and international instability. Relevant frameworks and global case studies were used effectively, although greater integration of recent regional incidents (e.g., Eastern Europe or Asia-Pacific) would have further strengthened the global applicability.

Criticality: Efforts were made to challenge the assumption that cyberterrorism is easily attributable or universally defined. There was a consistent attempt to explore competing viewpoints and highlight definitional ambiguity. However, I could have gone further in interrogating why states may benefit from maintaining blurred definitions or how academic disagreement weakens governance responses.

Use of Sources: A balanced range of academic, legal, and technical sources were used, drawing from both peer-reviewed and credible grey literature. While a few citations could have been more current, the breadth and relevance were generally strong. I made a conscious effort to prioritise interdisciplinary sources to reflect the topicâ€™s complexity.

Structure and Presentation: The overall structure followed a logical progression and included appropriate academic conventions. Future improvements would involve clearer subheadings to enhance readability and a more concise conclusion that reinforces key insights.

Academic Integrity: All citations were referenced using the correct Harvard format, and I ensured that all paraphrasing was accurate and appropriately attributed. I also used Turnitin to self-check originality prior to submission.

Overall, the literature review reflected meaningful engagement with a complex and emerging area of cybersecurity. Through this process, I developed stronger skills in critical synthesis, source evaluation, and articulating academic arguments across disciplinary boundaries.
