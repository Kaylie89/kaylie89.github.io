Evaluation of Statistical Exercises

This section involved practical application of statistical tests using SPSS, exploring hypotheses relevant to computing and cybersecurity contexts. Representative tests included a t-test, ANOVA, Pearson’s correlation, and Chi-square, each reinforcing different aspects of quantitative research.

The t-test exercise helped interpret mean differences, building confidence in evaluating intervention effects. The ANOVA scenario, though initially complex, proved particularly valuable by highlighting the importance of post-hoc testing in multi-group analysis. Correlation testing deepened understanding of linear relationships, while the Chi-square test provided insight into associations between categorical variables — a skill especially relevant for cybersecurity datasets like detection outcomes or system classifications.

Beyond performing the calculations, the most significant learning came from interpreting outputs and drawing conclusions. At times, assumptions such as normality or homogeneity were easy to overlook, so reviewing syntax and outputs critically became key. Writing interpretations in plain language for each output also strengthened academic communication skills.

Completing the full set of exercises helped reinforce core statistical concepts and highlighted the need for rigour and transparency in reporting findings. These exercises also informed design choices in the research proposal, such as considering parametric versus non-parametric options based on data characteristics.
