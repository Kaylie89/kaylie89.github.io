Evaluation of Statistical Exercises

This section showcases completed statistical exercises from Units 8 and 9, using Excel for data analysis. Each file contains embedded interpretations based on the outputs, demonstrating applied learning in key statistical techniques relevant to computing and cybersecurity contexts.

Exercises included t-tests, ANOVA (one-way and two-way), Pearson’s correlation, and the Chi-square test. These methods enabled practical testing of hypotheses and interpretation of relationships between variables—core to evidence-based cybersecurity research.

The t-test assessed group differences, such as comparing user response times under different conditions. One-way ANOVA extended this to comparisons across three or more groups. Two-way ANOVA explored interaction effects, such as simultaneous influence of design and condition on outcome.

Correlation analysis identified linear trends, for example between training duration and detection performance. Chi-square testing supported categorical comparisons, simulating scenarios like analysing user role and phishing awareness.

In Unit 9, I created effective charts for presenting data clearly to non-technical stakeholders. These included bar graphs, histograms, line charts, and pie charts, with an emphasis on clarity and ethical data communication.

These exercises reinforced skills in applying statistical tests appropriately, checking assumptions (e.g. normality, equal variances), and translating results into plain-English interpretations. They directly informed choices in my research proposal, including data planning, test selection, and how to justify methods to an academic or organisational audience.
